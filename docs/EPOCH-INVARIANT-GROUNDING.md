# Defining the Invariant — A Grounding Note

*A working note to stay aligned while reasoning about ψ, before any code gets written for
the constrained-manifold model. Drawn from a conversation; kept as a reference. The bar at the end is the thing to re-read when a candidate definition starts to feel right.*

---

## Why ψ comes first

ψ is the only element of the 6-tuple that is currently **load-bearing-by-claim but not
load-bearing-in-fact**. `S, Σ, δ, s₀, F` are all doing honest work in each derivation — they
are the standard automaton furniture, re-labeled, and that is fine. ψ is the one claimed to be
the *soul* of the thing, and the one that — as a static predicate — folds away into the state
set.

So until ψ is defined as something that **cannot be absorbed**, the whole framework rests on a
placeholder. And concretely: you cannot build QNM's `P_ψ` projection without knowing what it
projects onto. **The constraint surface *is* ψ.** Define the invariant, or you are training
toward an undefined target. The sequencing — *whole → invariant → QNM* — is right.

## The "collection as a whole" pull — interrogate it

Looking across all four derivations, the thing that is genuinely common is **not a shared ψ**.
Each has a different one: binding (`E<0`), decoherence, projection-membership, verification.
What is common is the *form*: in every case ψ is what is preserved-or-selected under the flow
such that identity survives. That is the invariant-about-invariants.

Here is the fork, and it matters which way the gravity is pulling:

- **One True ψ underneath them all** → a theory-of-everything. Admired, unfalsifiable.
- **A *schema*** — "ψ is whatever the dynamics leave fixed that constitutes the thing's
  continuity" → a definition. Usable.

The pull toward "the whole" could be either. **Name which one you are reaching for** before you
get far. The first explains everything and predicts nothing; the second is a tool.

## The discriminating test (keep this in your pocket)

The honest test for any ψ you write down: **can it distinguish trajectories that the pointwise
version cannot?** This is the lever you have already flagged in every doc.

Concretely — take two runs that reach the **same final state** by **different paths**, where one
preserved identity and one did not:

> a thing that *survived* vs. a thing that *died and was replaced with an identical copy*.

A pointwise ψ says they are the same; it only sees the endpoint. A genuine continuity-invariant
**must say they differ.**

> If your ψ cannot tell the Ship of Theseus from its replica, it is still the static one wearing
> a trajectory costume.

Run every candidate definition against this case.

## Watch for the definition that is true-by-construction

The failure mode in invariant-hunting is writing ψ so that it is **automatically satisfied** —
defining "the soul persists" as "the thing that persists." That is a tautology that explains
nothing.

A real ψ has to be the kind of thing that **can be false mid-trajectory**, not just at a clean
boundary. A boundary-only invariant is too easy; the trajectory is where the content lives.

## Why MWA / decoherence is the place to *find* it

Decoherence is the best teacher here — which is probably part of why MWA pulled at you. It is the
one substrate where identity is genuinely **history-laden**: whether a branch is "the same world"
depends on the decoherence record it laid down along its trajectory. So it is the natural place to
*discover* what a trajectory-dependent ψ looks like — and then carry that form back to QNM, where
you can actually **build** it and where it can actually **fail**.

(Recall the asymmetry: SOL and MWA *describe systems that already run*, so they cannot fail. The
constrained-manifold model is a thing you *construct* — it either trains within the constrained manifold and
stays on it, or it does not. That is a real pass/fail, which is why it is worth prioritizing over
more cosmological mappings.)

## The bar

Two sentences to re-read when a candidate ψ starts to feel right:

- **A definition that cannot be false somewhere is a name.**
- **A definition that survives the replica test is a tool.**

You will know which one you have got.
